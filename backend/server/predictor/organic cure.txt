1) Common tomato diseases in India: 
a) Early blight (Alternaria) - brown concentric leaf spots, fruit rot. 
b) Late blight (Phytophthora) - water-soaked lesions, rapid collapse in wet weather. 
c) Bacterial spot/ speck and bacterial wilt - small dark spots, wilting.
d) Fusarium and Verticillium wilt - one-sided yellowing, plant decline. 
e) Powdery mildew - white powder on leaves.

2) Prevention: 
start with healthy seed/seedlings, use resistant varieties, practice 2-3 year rotation away from Solanaceae , keep good plant spacing and ventilation, avoid overhead watering, maintain healthy soil with well-decomposed compost or FYM and use Trichoderma/Bacillus bio-inoculants.
Remove and destroy infected plants and sanitize tools. Monitor fields regularly and act at first symptoms.

3) Best Practices for Organic Disease Control and Soil Health :
a) Cultural: use resistant varieties, rotate crops 2-3 years, remove and destroy circulation (prune, stake), avoid overhead watering, plant in well-drained beds and space plants properly. b) Soil and biological: apply compost, well-decomposed FYM and Trichoderma or mycorrhiza bio-inoculants to improve soil health and suppress pathogens. 
c) Organic sprays: c1) For fungal diseases copper fungicide spray (organic-approved) or wettable sulphur as label allows; use neem oil or potassium bicarbonate for powdery mildew and minor fungal control. c2) For bacterial diseases - avoid copper overuse but apply copper-based bactericide early, use neem leaf extract and bio-products (e.g., Bacillus subtilis/Bacillus     amyloliquefaciens) . c3) For wilts soil solarization, grafting onto resistant rootstocks, seed treatment with Trichoderma.
d) Integrated timing: start preventive sprays at first sign of humidity/stress and repeat as label recommends; increase sanitation during monsoon.


4) Early blight (Alternaria concentric brown leaf/fruit spots): Remove and destroy infected leaves and fruits. Improve air flow by pruning lower leaves and staking; avoid overhead irrigation and water early morning. Apply soil health measures: well-decomposed FYM or compost, Trichoderma seed/soil treatment, and crop rotation 2-3 years away from solanaceous crops. Foliar control: alternate copper-based fungicide (organic-approved) with a bio-fungicide containing Bacillus subtilis or Trichoderma; use neem oil or potassium bicarbonate as supplementary sprays. Start preventively at first signs of wet weather and repeat at 7-14 day intervals depending on disease pressure. Sanitize tools and destroy volunteer plants.

5) Late blight (Phytophthora water-soaked lesions, rapid collapse): This disease spreads fast in cool, wet weather so act early. Remove and burn severely infected plants; avoid moving through wet fields to limit spread. Use raised beds and fast drainage, space plants widely and prune to increase sunlight. Soil/plant health: compost, well-drained soil, and use resistant varieties where available. Spray preventively when conditions favor disease: copper fungicide (organic-approved) alternated with a biological like Bacillus amyloliquefaciens ; apply potassium bicarbonate for small outbreaks. Shorten spray interval (5-7 days) during persistent rains. Do not compost heavily infected material; destroy it away from the field.

6) Bacterial wilt ( Ralstonia ) and bacterial spot/speck:
Start with clean, disease-free seed/seedlings and practice strict field sanitation. Solarize soil or apply organic amendments (compost, FYM) and Trichoderma to improve suppressiveness. Remove and destroy infected plants immediately; avoid working when plants are wet. Foliar: copper bactericide can reduce spread but use carefully to avoid phytotoxicity and resistance; combine with neem leaf extract or bioagents (Bacillus spp.). For wilt management, consider grafting susceptible scions onto wilts-tolerant rootstocks and maintain balanced irrigation to reduce plant stress. Rotate with non-host crops and control weeds that host bacteria.

7) Powdery mildew: remove heavily infected leaves, improve air flow and sunlight, spray potassium bicarbonate (or baking soda solution) or neem oil early and repeat every 7-10 days; sulfur
(organic-approved) can be used if label allows and conditions suit.

8) Fusarium & Verticillium wilt: no reliable foliar cure: prevent via resistant varieties and grafting onto tolerant rootstocks, solarize soil where possible, apply plenty of compost and Trichoderma/beneficial microbes, treat seed/roots with Trichoderma before transplanting, avoid waterlogging and reduce plant stress.

9) Bacterial spot/speck: rogue and destroy infected plants, avoid working when plants are wet, use copper-based bactericide sparingly (follow label) combined with neem extracts or Bacillus sprays to reduce spread; ensure clean seed, control volunteers and alternate hosts.


10) Panchakavya Overview:
Panchakavya is a traditional Indian organic fertilizer made from five key ingredients derived from cow products. It is widely used in organic farming for enhancing soil fertility and promoting plant growth.
anic 
Key Ingredients:
Cow Dung: Rich in nutrients and beneficial microorganisms.
Cow Urine: Contains nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, and other essential nutrients.
Milk: Provides proteins and beneficial bacteria.
Curd (Yogurt): A source of beneficial microorganisms that aid in soil health.
Ghee (Clarified Butter): Enhances the nutrient absorption in plants.

Benefits of Panchakavya:
Improves Soil Fertility: Enriches the soil with essential nutrients and enhances microbial activity. Promotes Plant Growth: Stimulates growth and increases crop yield. Enhances Disease Resistance: Strengthens plants' immunity against pests and diseases.
Eco-Friendly: A natural alternative to chemical fertilizers, promoting sustainable agriculture.

Preparation Method:
Collect Ingredients: Gather fresh cow dung, cow urine, milk, curd, and ghee.
Mixing: Combine all ingredients in a clean container.
Fermentation: Allow the mixture to ferment for about 7-10 days, stirring occasionally.
Dilution: Before application, dilute the fermented mixture with water (1:10 ratio).
Application: Use as a foliar spray or soil drench for plants.

Usage
Foliar Spray: Apply directly to the leaves for quick absorption.
Soil Application: Mix with soil during planting or as a top dressing.

Conclusion:
Panchakavya is a valuable tool for organic farmers, promoting healthy soil and robust plant growth while being environmentally friendly. Its use aligns with sustainable agricultural practices, contributing to the overall health of ecosystems.


11) a)Preparation & steps of Panchakavya:
Ingredients Required
Cow Dung: 5 liters
Cow Urine: 3 liters
Milk: 2 liters
Curd (Yogurt): 2 liters
Ghee (Clarified Butter): 1 liter

Steps for Preparation:
Collect Fresh Ingredients: Ensure that all ingredients are fresh and sourced from healthy cows.

Mixing:
In a clean container, combine the cow dung, cow urine, milk, curd, and ghee.
Stir the mixture thoroughly to ensure even distribution of all components.
Fermentation:
Cover the container with a cloth to allow air circulation while preventing contamination.
Let the mixture ferment for about 7 to 10 days. Stir it occasionally to enhance the fermentation process.
Final Mixture:
After fermentation, the mixture will have a pleasant smell and a thick consistency.
It is now ready for application.

b)Application of Panchakavya:
Dilution:
Before applying, dilute the fermented Panchakavya with water in a 1:10 ratio (1 part Panchakavya to 10 parts water).

Methods of Application:
a)Foliar Spray:
Use a sprayer to apply the diluted Panchakavya directly onto the leaves of the plants.
This method allows for quick absorption of nutrients and beneficial microorganisms.

b)Soil Application:
Mix the diluted Panchakavya into the soil during planting or as a top dressing around the base of the plants.
This helps improve soil fertility and promotes root development.

Frequency of Application:
Foliar Spray: Apply every 15 to 30 days during the growing season.

Soil Application: Use at the time of planting and repeat every 2 to 3 months for established crops.

Conclusion
Panchakavya is a powerful organic fertilizer that enhances soil health and promotes plant growth. Its preparation is straightforward, and its application can significantly improve agricultural productivity while adhering to sustainable farming practices.


12) Amutha Karaisal Overview:
Amutha Karaisal is a traditional organic fertilizer used in agriculture, particularly in Tamil Nadu, India. It is known for its effectiveness in enhancing soil fertility and promoting healthy plant growth.

Key Ingredients:
Amutha Karaisal is typically made from the following components:

Cow Dung: Provides essential nutrients and beneficial microorganisms.
Cow Urine: Rich in nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, it helps in plant growth.
Plant Extracts: Various local plants may be used, such as neem leaves, which have pest-repelling properties.
Water: Used to dilute the mixture for application.

Preparation Method:
Collect Ingredients: Gather fresh cow dung, cow urine, and selected plant materials.

Mixing: Combine the cow dung and cow urine in a container. Add the plant extracts to the mixture.

Fermentation: Allow the mixture to ferment for a period (usually around 7-10 days) in a shaded area, stirring occasionally.

Dilution: After fermentation, dilute the mixture with water before application.

Benefits of Amutha Karaisal:
Enhances Soil Fertility: Enriches the soil with essential nutrients and improves microbial activity.

Promotes Healthy Plant Growth: Stimulates root development and overall plant vigor.

Pest Control: The inclusion of certain plant extracts can help deter pests naturally.

Eco-Friendly: A sustainable alternative to chemical fertilizers, promoting organic farming practices.

Application:
Foliar Spray: Apply the diluted Amutha Karaisal directly to the leaves for quick nutrient absorption.

Soil Application: Mix into the soil during planting or as a top dressing for established crops.

Conclusion:
Amutha Karaisal is a valuable organic fertilizer that supports sustainable agriculture by improving soil health and promoting robust plant growth. Its traditional preparation methods and natural ingredients make it an excellent choice for organic farmers.

13) EM - Bokashi:
Bokashi is a type of compost prepared by fermentation of waste organic matter with the help of EM. Bokashi is mainly used for improving the fertility status of soil and for enhancing the degradation of crop residue.

* Collect sufficient quantity of different organic matter (such as rice bran, fish meal, animal waste etc) equivalent to 150 lit drum volume.
* Mix 150gm of jaggary and 50 ml of EM in 15 lit of water.
* Mix this solution with organic waste thoroughly in such a way that entire contents get uniformly moistened.
* Grind 2.3 kg of fresh green weeds to a coarse paste. Dilute with 14 lit of water.
* Dissolve 42 gm of jaggary in some water and mix with weed suspension.
* Add 420ml of EM
* Transfer the contents to a plastic drum and with the help of a thick plastic sheet
* Transfer the contents in a plastic bag and seal the bag.
* To ensure the anaerobic conditions put this bag into another polythene bag and seal
* Allow the contents to ferment for 3-4 days in a cool shade place
* Bokashi will be ready after 4 days.
* This can be used immediately.
* In plastic air tight bags Bokashi can be stored up to 6 months.

14) How to use Bokashi:
Bokashi can be used directly as compost in poor fertility soils. It can also be used along with the crop residues. For 0.1 ha mix 100-150 kg Bokashi with sufficient quantity of finely chopped crop residue. Spread this mixture over 0.1 ha area and mixed with soil a day before sowing. Spraying of 5-10 lit of 1:500 diluted simple EM-solution over this mixture can further boost the degradation process. By using Bokashi+crop residue+ EM-solution the requirement of compost can be dispensed with. This can save lot of labour, time and space required for compost process.

Application of EM formulations at the time of land preparation:

Dilute 5-10 lit of simple EM solution in 50-100 lit of water and sprinkle/spray over 0.1 ha of land, when soil is wet a day before sowing.

a)For seed treatment:

Soak seeds for 5-6 hrs in 1 : 100 fold diluted EM solution and sow immediately.

b)As foliar/ soil spray:

After seedling emergence, 1 : 1000 diluted EM solution or FPE should be sprayed at the rate of 500 lit per ha, 4-5 times at an interval of 7-10 days. In fast growing crops such as vegetables, spraying should be done twice a week. In transplanted crops 1 : 500 diluted FPE can be sprayed after 5 days of transplanting @ 750-1000 lit per ha.

c)For soil enrichment:

For every 0.1 ha mix 100-150 kg Bokashi with crop residue and mix with soil just before sowing. Simple EM solution @ 5-10 lit can also be used as spray over this residue-Bokashi mix. Spraying the soil with 5-10 lit of FPE mixed in 500-1000 lit of water per ha also add to the fertility of the soil.
